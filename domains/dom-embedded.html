§ 01 — Overview
Compute that lives somewhere

Embedded engineering is what happens when you take everything else and force it through a 32-bit microcontroller, a 2 W power budget, and a bus on the other side of the satellite. It rewards designs that respect their constraints — and punishes anything that doesn't.

The work here spans autonomous flying platforms, planetary rovers, quadruped mechanisms and CubeSat avionics — different bodies, but the same underlying problem: sense, decide, act, without dropping a frame.

§ 02 — Sub-areas
What this covers

SUB-01
Autonomous Navigation
Locomotion algorithms and control laws for aerial and ground platforms — closing the perception–planning loop on-board.
SUB-02
CubeSat Avionics
EPS, OBC, comms and ADCS subsystems sized for the bus, the orbit and the team building it.
SUB-03
Sensor Fusion
IMU, sun-sensor and camera streams combined into pose estimates that survive resource budgets and noise.
SUB-04
Real-Time Data Pipelines
Embedded data paths from sensor → processing → telemetry — bounded latency, predictable behaviour.
SUB-05
Microcontroller Integration
System integration on resource-constrained MCUs — peripherals, scheduling and power-aware firmware.
SUB-06
Mechanical Co-design
Working alongside the structural team — quadruped mechanisms, CubeSat enclosures, payload accommodation.
