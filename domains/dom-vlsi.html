§ 01 — Overview
Where ideas become silicon

A clean compute idea is only worth as much as the chip it ends up on. VLSI is the discipline of dragging a high-level architecture through every stage — RTL, simulation, synthesis, place-and-route — without losing what made it interesting in the first place.

The work here treats power, performance and area as design variables, not afterthoughts: arithmetic units re-shaped to match precision budgets, datapaths balanced for timing, and every micro- architectural choice justified against the PPA frontier.

§ 02 — Sub-areas
What this covers

SUB-01
RTL Design
Synthesizable Verilog / SystemVerilog for arithmetic blocks, pipelines and control state machines.
SUB-02
Functional Verification
Directed and constrained-random testbenches, golden-reference comparisons, and assertion-based checks.
SUB-03
Architecture Optimisation
PPA-driven trade studies across pipeline depth, parallelism and precision — finding the right operating point per workload.
SUB-04
Low-Power Design
Clock-gating, operand isolation, and precision-adaptive datapaths for energy-constrained inference.
SUB-05
Flow Integration
End-to-end Cadence / Vivado / Synopsys flow — from RTL through synthesis to gate-level signoff.
SUB-06
Arithmetic Units
Custom adders, multipliers and approximate primitives — the building blocks behind every accelerator on the bench.
