§ DOM-01 · Active Domain · Primary focus area

FPGA & Reconfigurable Computing

Programmable hardware as a compute substrate — turning Python-trained neural networks into custom IP blocks that run on Artix-7 silicon through HLS, RTL refinement and SoC integration.

FocusHW–SW co-design
ToolingVivado · Vitis HLS
TargetsArtix-7 XC7A200T
Status● Active research

§ 01 — Overview
The question

Reconfigurable computing sits in a useful gap. ASICs give you peak efficiency but commit silicon to one workload; CPUs and GPUs are flexible but pay for it in joules per inference. FPGAs let you trade between the two — re-fabricating the data path to match the model in front of you.

The work here is about how to actually close that gap in practice: choosing the right compute primitive (LUT, log-domain, approximate), wrapping it in a clean AXI4 SoC, and pushing it through Vivado without losing timing.

§ 02 — Sub-areas
What this covers

SUB-01
High-Level Synthesis
Algorithmic descriptions in C/C++ compiled to RTL via Vivado HLS. Pragma-driven optimisation of pipelines, dataflow and memory partitioning.
SUB-02
RTL Architecture
Hand-tuned Verilog where HLS doesn't cut it — clock-domain crossings, FIFO sizing and pipelined arithmetic for predictable timing.
SUB-03
AXI4 SoC Integration
Memory-mapped AXI4 between custom accelerator IP and embedded ARM cores; DMA-driven activations and bitstream-baked metadata.
SUB-04
PPA Optimisation
Power, performance and area treated as first-class design knobs. Vivado reports parsed into sweep dashboards for repeatable exploration.
SUB-05
Quantisation & Approximate Compute
Sensitivity-driven quantisation pipelines that pick precision per layer — the question is which layers tolerate which approximations.
SUB-06
Verification
Bit-accurate C++ models paired with cocotb testbenches; coverage-driven regression on representative DNN slices.
