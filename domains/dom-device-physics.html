Active Domain DOM-02

Semiconductor Device Physics

Tooling: Silvaco TCAD (Athena + Atlas) Focus: Beyond-CMOS, GAA, sub-5 nm

The physics of how transistors work — and how they fail — as silicon scaling moves from FinFETs into Gate-All-Around territory. The goal is simulation-driven insight into what geometry and process choices enable sub-5 nm logic that still delivers useful current with manageable leakage.

Overview

Post-FinFET transistors — GAA nanosheets, MBCFETs — offer better electrostatic control but introduce a wider design space. Geometry (nanosheet thickness, pitch, gate length), doping profiles, and process flow all interact non-linearly in ways that are hard to reason about without simulation.

The work here uses Silvaco TCAD — Athena for process simulation, Atlas for device characterisation — to explore that space systematically, identify the geometric levers that dominate short-channel immunity and drive current, and cross-validate against published references.

TCAD Approach

  • Process Simulation (Athena) — Multi-step process scripts covering epi growth, oxide deposition, and gate stack definition. Parametric to enable structured sweeps.
  • Device Characterisation (Atlas) — DC Id–Vg, Id–Vd sweeps; subthreshold slope; DIBL. Drift-diffusion transport with mobility models calibrated for thin-body silicon.
  • Geometry Sweeps — Nanosheet thickness, pitch, gate length, and S/D doping varied to map their combined effect on Ion/Ioff.
  • SCE Quantification — DIBL, Vt roll-off, and subthreshold slope tracked across the sweep — finding the geometry window that holds at scale.
  • Optimisation — Pareto curves of Ion vs. Ioff to guide selection of stack geometries that maintain drive current without sacrificing leakage.

Active Research

  • R-02 — MBCFET Structure Modeling & Physics: GAA Multi-Bridge-Channel FET simulation in Silvaco TCAD, targeting sub-5 nm nodes. Ongoing.
