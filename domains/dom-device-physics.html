§ 01 — Overview
Where the silicon goes next

Classical CMOS scaling stopped working a decade ago. What replaced it — FinFET, then GAA nanosheets, then multi-bridge channels — is a sequence of geometric tricks to keep the gate in control of a channel that's getting smaller than the physics it's made of.

This domain studies that boundary. Where does electrostatic control break down? How thin can a sheet get before mobility collapses? Which stack geometries hold up when you push them past the next node?

§ 02 — Sub-areas
What this covers

SUB-01
GAA FET Architectures
Gate-all-around variants — nanowire and nanosheet topologies — and the trade-offs they introduce in drive current vs leakage.
SUB-02
MBCFET / Multi-Bridge Channels
Stacked-sheet successors to single-nanosheet GAAs — Samsung's roadmap device for the post-3 nm era.
SUB-03
Process Simulation
Silvaco Athena process flow — epi growth, oxide, gate stack — scripted parametrically to enable geometry sweeps.
SUB-04
Device Characterisation
Atlas DC sims — Id–Vg, Id–Vd, subthreshold slope, DIBL — with mobility models calibrated for thin-body silicon.
SUB-05
Short-Channel Effects
Drain-induced barrier lowering, threshold roll-off and subthreshold leakage as channel length pushes single-digit nm.
SUB-06
Carrier Transport
Drift-diffusion regimes vs ballistic effects in deeply scaled bodies — and where compact models begin to fail.
