§ R-02 · Active Research · Oct 2025 – Present

MBCFET Structure Modeling & Physics

TCAD-driven device study of the Multi-Bridge-Channel FET — Samsung's GAA evolution of the nanosheet transistor — aimed at understanding what controls Ion/Ioff and short-channel immunity in sub-5 nm logic.

TypeDevice-level TCAD study
ToolchainSilvaco Athena + Atlas
Target nodeSub-5 nm
FocusSCE · DIBL · subthreshold

§ 01 — Overview
Why MBCFET

As CMOS shrinks past FinFET, GAA architectures take over because wrap-around gates restore electrostatic control of the channel. The MBCFET stacks multiple nanosheet bridges in parallel to widen the effective channel without growing the footprint — but the design space (sheet thickness, pitch, spacing, doping) is vast and non-linear.

This work uses Silvaco TCAD — Athena for process flow, Atlas for device characterisation — to scan that design space and identify the levers that matter for SCE and current drive in the sub-5 nm regime.

§ 02 — Approach
Simulation method

PROC-01
Process Flow (Athena)
Multi-step process simulation — epi growth, oxide, gate stack — assembled as a parametric script to enable sweeps.
DEV-02
Device Sim (Atlas)
DC characterisation: Id–Vg, Id–Vd, subthreshold slope, DIBL. Drift-diffusion with mobility models calibrated for thin-body silicon.
GEO-03
Geometry Sweeps
Nanosheet thickness, sheet pitch, gate length and S/D doping varied systematically to map their effect on Ion/Ioff.
SCE-04
Short-Channel Effects
DIBL, threshold-voltage roll-off, subthreshold slope quantified across the sweep — to find the geometry envelope that holds at scale.
OPT-05
Optimisation
Pareto curves of Ion vs Ioff guide selection of stack geometries that maintain drive current without sacrificing leakage.
VAL-06
Cross-Validation
Selected operating points cross-checked against analytical models and published Synopsys Sentaurus references for sanity.

§ 03 — Why it matters
What this unlocks

A clean understanding of which geometric parameters dominate MBCFET behaviour is a prerequisite for any compact model that wants to predict circuit performance at sub-5 nm. This work feeds directly into downstream questions about how next-generation logic — including the FPGA work on Sheet R-01 — will scale on post-FinFET silicon.
