Ongoing R-02

MBCFET Structure Modeling & Physics

Active Research Oct 2025 – Present Silvaco TCAD (Athena + Atlas)

A TCAD-driven device study of the Multi-Bridge-Channel FET — Samsung's GAA evolution of the nanosheet transistor — aimed at understanding the geometric parameters that control Ion/Ioff and short-channel immunity at sub-5 nm logic nodes.

TypeDevice-level TCAD study
ToolchainSilvaco Athena (process) + Atlas (device)
Target nodeSub-5 nm
FocusShort-channel effects · DIBL · subthreshold slope

Why MBCFET?

As CMOS shrinks past FinFET, Gate-All-Around architectures take over because the wrap-around gate restores electrostatic control of the channel. The MBCFET stacks multiple nanosheet bridges in parallel to widen the effective channel without growing the footprint — but the design space (sheet thickness, pitch, spacing, doping profile) is vast and non-linear.

This work uses Silvaco TCAD to scan that design space and identify which geometric parameters dominate short-channel immunity and current drive in the sub-5 nm regime.

Simulation Method

  • Process Flow — Athena: Multi-step process simulation (epi growth, oxide deposition, gate stack) assembled as a parametric script to enable structured sweeps.
  • Device Simulation — Atlas: DC characterisation (Id–Vg, Id–Vd, subthreshold slope, DIBL). Drift-diffusion transport with mobility models calibrated for thin-body silicon.
  • Geometry Sweeps: Nanosheet thickness, sheet pitch, gate length, and S/D doping varied systematically to map their combined effect on Ion/Ioff.
  • Short-Channel Effect Analysis: DIBL, threshold-voltage roll-off, and subthreshold slope quantified across the sweep — to find the geometry envelope that holds at scale.
  • Optimisation: Pareto curves of Ion vs. Ioff guide selection of stack geometries that maintain drive current without sacrificing leakage.
  • Cross-Validation: Selected operating points cross-checked against analytical models and published Sentaurus references.

Why It Matters

A clean understanding of which geometric parameters dominate MBCFET behaviour is a prerequisite for compact models that predict circuit performance at sub-5 nm. This work feeds directly into downstream questions about how next-generation logic — including post-FinFET FPGA fabric — will scale in terms of power density and switching speed.
