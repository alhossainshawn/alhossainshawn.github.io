§ P-01 · Final Year Design Project · Jan 2025 – Dec 2025

Autonomous Aerial Survey Platform

An end-to-end pipeline for autonomous aerial survey — flight simulation in the loop, on-board image processing, and mission-level automation closing the loop between sensor and plan.

TypeAutonomous aerial platform
ClassFinal Year Design Project
DomainSim · CV · Autonomy
InstitutionBRAC University · EEE

§ 01 — Brief
The problem

Survey drones are still mostly piloted — a human flies the grid, a human sifts the imagery. This project asks how much of that loop can be moved into the platform itself, by combining a flight simulator (for training and verification), a real-time image processing stack (for on-board interpretation), and a planner (for adapting the survey while it is happening).

§ 02 — Pipeline
The three layers

SIM
Flight Simulation
Drone dynamics simulated in the loop — used to develop and verify the control law and the survey planner before any field test.
CV
Image Processing
On-board imagery pipeline tuned for survey workloads — feature extraction and target detection feeding the autonomy layer.
AUT
Automation
Mission planner that decides where to look next based on what the image stack reports — closing the loop in flight.

§ 03 — Why it mattered
What it taught me

Closing a perception–planning loop end-to-end forced honesty about every layer. The simulator caught what the controller would not; the image stack caught what the simulator could not; and the planner had to be conservative about what it could trust from either. Useful preparation for the kind of co-design that hardware accelerators for inference workloads also demand — knowing where the slack lives.
