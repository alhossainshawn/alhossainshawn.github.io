Autonomous Aerial Survey Platform

Final Year Design Project Jan 2025 – Dec 2025 BRAC University · EEE

An end-to-end pipeline for autonomous aerial survey — flight simulation in the loop, on-board image processing, and mission-level automation closing the loop between sensor and plan.

TypeAutonomous aerial platform
ClassFinal Year Design Project
PlatformROS · PX4
PayloadThermal / HD imaging
FirmwareC++ · Python

Project Brief

Survey drones are still mostly piloted — a human flies the grid, a human sifts the imagery. This project asks how much of that loop can be moved into the platform itself, by combining a flight simulator (for training and verification), a real-time image processing stack (for on-board interpretation), and a mission planner (for adapting the survey while it is happening).

The Three-Layer Pipeline

  • Flight Simulation (SIM) — Drone dynamics simulated in the loop, used to develop and verify the control law and survey planner before any field test. PX4 SITL with ROS integration.
  • Image Processing (CV) — On-board imagery pipeline tuned for survey workloads: feature extraction and target detection feeding the autonomy layer. Thermal and visible-spectrum fusion.
  • Mission Automation (AUT) — Mission planner that decides where to look next based on what the image stack reports — closing the loop in flight, adapting waypoints based on findings.

What It Taught Me

Closing a perception–planning loop end-to-end forces honesty about every layer. The simulator catches what the controller would not; the image stack catches what the simulator cannot; and the planner must be conservative about what it can trust from either. This kind of co-design thinking — knowing where the slack lives — transfers directly to hardware accelerator design for inference workloads.
