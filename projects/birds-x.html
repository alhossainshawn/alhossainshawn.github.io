BIRDS-X APRS Payload

Payload Engineer Jan 2023 – Apr 2023 Phase 1 Winner — BIRDS-X Competition

An APRS payload designed around energy-transition messaging — built for the BIRDS-X national space payload competition, where the team won the Phase 1 round.

TypeAPRS satellite payload
ResultPhase 1 Winner
ThemeEnergy transition / renewables
PCB ToolAltium Designer
ProtocolAX.25 / APRS Digipeater

The Payload

The BIRDS-X programme asks teams to design APRS payloads that amplify a chosen social or scientific theme. Ours centred on the energy transition — planning, management, and renewable adoption — translating a policy question into a small radio artefact that could fly.

The work spanned the full payload stack: hardware on PCB, mechanical accommodation in 3D, orbit-aware operations planning, and image processing for the data the payload would carry.

What We Built

  • Payload Hardware (PCB) — Schematic, layout, and DFM on the payload PCB, sized for the BIRDS-X form factor and integrated into the host satellite bus. Designed in Altium Designer.
  • Mechanical (3D) — Mechanical accommodation modelled in 3D; clearance, fasteners, and thermal mounts checked against the bus envelope.
  • Orbital Simulation (ORB) — Pass timing and station contact windows simulated to drive a realistic operations plan.
  • Image Processing (CV) — Image-side pipeline tuned for the chosen theme, turning sensor data into things worth downlinking.

Phase 1 Win

The Phase 1 win validated the design choices — a focused theme, a clean hardware stack, and an operations plan rooted in real orbit numbers. It also primed the larger CubeSat work that followed (Project P-02) by giving the team a working language across four engineering disciplines simultaneously.
