§ P-03 · Payload Engineer · Jan 2023 – Apr 2023

BIRDS-X APRS Payload

An APRS payload designed around energy-transition messaging — built for the BIRDS-X competition, where we won the Phase 1 round.

TypeAPRS satellite payload
ResultPhase 1 Winner
ThemeEnergy transition / renewables
ToolchainPCB · 3D · Orbital sim · CV

§ 01 — Brief
The payload

The BIRDS-X programme asks teams to design APRS payloads that amplify a chosen social or scientific theme. Ours centred on the energy transition — planning, management and renewable adoption — translating a policy question into a small radio artefact that could fly.

The work spanned the full payload stack: hardware on PCB, mechanical accommodation in 3D, orbit-aware operations planning, and image processing for the data the payload would carry.

§ 02 — Stack
What we built

PCB
Payload Hardware
Schematic, layout, and DFM on the payload PCB — sized for the BIRDS-X form factor and integrated into the host satellite bus.
3D
Mechanical
Mechanical accommodation modelled in 3D — clearance, fasteners, and thermal mounts checked against the bus envelope.
ORB
Orbital Simulation
Pass timing and station contact windows simulated to drive a realistic ops plan — what to transmit when.
CV
Image Processing
Image-side pipeline tuned for the chosen theme — turning sensor data into things worth downlinking.

§ 03 — Outcome
Phase 1 winner

The Phase 1 win validated the design choices — a focused theme, a clean stack, and an ops plan rooted in real orbit numbers. It also primed the larger CubeSat work that followed (Sheet P-02) by giving the team a working language across the four disciplines.
