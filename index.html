Active research

Two open threads: a hardware accelerator targeting edge AI workloads, and a TCAD study of multi-bridge-channel devices for sub-5 nm logic.

R-01 · Feb 2026 – Present In Review

Hybrid Neural Accelerator

A reconfigurable LUT / logarithmic compute engine for AI inference, prototyped on the Artix-7 XC7A200T.

  • Vivado HLS compute engine optimized for PPA across mixed precisions.
  • RTL prototype with timing closure and resource mapping on the XC7A200T.
  • AXI4-based co-design between custom IP and ARM cores.

R-02 · Oct 2025 – Present Ongoing

MBCFET Modeling

GAA Multi-Bridge-Channel FET simulation in Silvaco TCAD, targeting sub-5 nm technology nodes.

  • Analysis of short-channel effects, DIBL and subthreshold behavior.
  • Nanosheet dimension optimization to maximize Ion/Ioff ratios.
  • Process-flow exploration across stack geometries.

Research interests

Three connected threads: device physics that defines what is possible, hardware that turns it into compute, and embedded systems that put it into the world.

DOM-01

FPGA & Reconfigurable Computing

  • Hardware accelerator design on Artix-7: Vivado HLS to bitstream.
  • AXI4-based co-design between custom IP and embedded ARM cores.
  • Bridging Python-trained models to Verilog hardware for edge inference.

Semiconductor Device Modeling & Physics
